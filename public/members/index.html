How decisions are made

Mycorrhizal Governance.

Holding Hyphae does not give you voting power. mycoTrust does — and it cannot be bought, only earned through ongoing relational depth. It decays without participation, just as mycelial connections wither without flow.

1
Voice grows through depth, not accumulation
mycoTrust is soulbound (non-transferable). It accrues from owner-awarded contributions, peer endorsements from other trusted members, and active participation.
2
Connections fade without flow
mycoTrust decays at 1% per month of inactivity. A member who hasn't sporeed, drawn, gifted, or endorsed in 100 months returns to zero — the network breathes.
3
Peers feed each other
Each member with active trust receives a monthly endorsement budget to recognise contributions from others. This makes governance horizontal, not top-down.
4
Decisions fruit, they don't win
Proposals go through composting (open discussion), sporulation (consent voting — 75% approve, zero strong objections), then fruiting (implementation). Inspired by sociocracy and Ostrom's commons principles.
5
Voice influences, never gates
mycoTrust matters for governance — but never to access products, ceremonies, or events. Hyphae open those doors. The two systems are intentionally distinct.
Entering the network

Becoming a thread.

1
Install MetaMask (or any EIP-1193 wallet — Rainbow, Coinbase, Trust). Free browser extension. ~5 minutes. metamask.io
2
Add Polygon network — chain ID 137. Gas fees are fractions of a cent. The wallet does this automatically the first time you connect to fungai.art.
3
Reach out — email robin@fungai.art with your wallet address and a few sentences about why you'd like to join the network. Founding cohort is invitation-based.
4
Receive your first sporing — Robin sporeed you 144 Hyphae as a founding member. You're now part of the network. Connect on this page to see your balance.
5
Contribute, draw, return — the network is alive only when threads carry flow. Show up to a foraging circle. Help at an event. Spend your Hyphae on a tincture. Gift some to a fellow member. The pattern is the practice.
